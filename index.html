Sisig Pampanga

Sisig refers to a finely chopped pork dish of Filipino origin. This recipe uses pork belly and maskara (pig face). It is popular in the Philippines and is now gaining popularity worldwide. It can be consumed as an appetizer and a main dish.

Bicol Express Bicol

Bicol Express is pure comfort food. With pork cubes cooked in coconut milk and chili peppers, it's rich, creamy, spicy and delicious. Serve with steamed rice for a hearty and big flavored meal.

Tamales Cavite Tamales is made from ground peanuts, cornflour or galapong topped with cut boiled eggs, garbanzos and shredded chicken and pork. Then wrapped with banana leaves and steamed for a couple of minutes.
Buko Pie Laguna Buko pie is a traditional Filipino pastry invented in 1965. The filling is made with a mixture of fresh young coconut, coconut juice, and milks and thickened with cornstarch, then encased in a tender pie crust.
Pancit Palabok Malabon Pancit Palabok is another popular and favorite pancit or noodle dish among Filipinos. It is made of rice noodles with thick yellow-orange sauce, and various toppings like shrimps, flakes of smoked fish, pork chicharon, hard-boiled eggs, spring onions, squid rings, fried garlic, among other things.
